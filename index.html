Scene Close Reading

Marmeladov’s Tavern

Raskolnikov enters trying to remain cold and theoretical, but the tavern scene forces him into proximity with human suffering.

What The Space Does

The tavern saturates him with poverty, confession, humiliation, and pity. It weakens his fantasy that he can think beyond ordinary moral feeling.

Why It Matters

Marmeladov’s speech reveals that Raskolnikov is not detached from suffering at all. His compassion keeps returning, and that instability becomes central to the novel.
